Understanding the Brain

The brain is the control center of the human body, responsible for thought, emotion, memory, and coordination. It consists of billions of neurons and glial cells, organized into lobes and structures that manage everything from motor control to complex reasoning.

What is a Brain Tumor?

A brain tumor is an abnormal mass of tissue in which cells grow uncontrollably. Tumors can be benign (non-cancerous) or malignant (cancerous). They may originate in the brain (primary) or spread from other parts of the body (secondary).

Common Causes & Risk Factors
  • Genetic mutations or hereditary syndromes
  • Exposure to high levels of radiation
  • Family history of brain tumors
  • Immune system disorders
  • Age and gender (some tumors are age- or sex-specific)

Why Early Detection Matters

Detecting brain tumors early improves survival and treatment outcomes. MRI scans combined with AI-powered detection platforms can identify tumors at earlier stages, enabling doctors to act faster and more effectively with surgery, chemo, or radiotherapy.
